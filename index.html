Workforce Overview
High-level snapshot of IBM's HR dataset — 1,470 employees across 3 departments
🧠 Section Summary
This section gives a bird's-eye view of IBM's entire workforce. Out of 1,470 employees, 237 have left — a 16.1% attrition rate, well above the healthy industry benchmark of 10–12%. The workforce skews young (peak age 30–34), is majority male (60%), and is heavily concentrated in Research & Development. These overview charts set the context for all deeper analysis in the sections that follow.
💡 Key Takeaways
  • 16.1% attrition — 4–6 percentage points above the healthy 10–12% benchmark (34–61% higher in relative terms)
  • R&D is the largest dept with 961 employees (65% of workforce)
  • Workforce peaks at ages 30–34 — a young, mobile talent base
  • Nearly 1 in 3 employees works overtime — systemic burnout risk
  • Gender is relatively balanced: 60% male, 40% female

Performance Analysis
Performance rating distribution, its link to attrition, training effectiveness, and job satisfaction by role
🧠 Section Summary
IBM uses only two performance ratings in practice: 3 (Excellent) and 4 (Outstanding) — meaning every employee is rated above-average. This compressed scale means performance scores cannot differentiate attrition risk (both groups leave at ~16%). More strikingly, training frequency has zero impact on performance — ratings remain flat at ~3.15 regardless of how many sessions an employee attends. Job satisfaction across all roles is uniformly below the midpoint at ~2.7/4.
💡 Key Takeaways
  • 84.6% are rated "Excellent" (3) — compressed scale limits insight
  • Attrition rate is nearly identical for Rating 3 (16.1%) and Rating 4 (16.4%)
  • Training has no measurable effect on performance scores
  • Healthcare Reps report highest satisfaction (2.79); HR reports lowest (2.56)
  • All roles score below 2.8/4 — universal satisfaction problem
